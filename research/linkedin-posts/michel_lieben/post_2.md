I just hit 70,000 LinkedIn followers. This got me to $7M+ ARR.

Here's every tool I use to grow my audience:

1. Ideation

→ Taplio

I browse their in-app feed to find outperforming content in my niche (or adjacent niches).

You can input keywords such as: "GTM", "Claude Code" or "Outbound" and then filter posts that:

- were published in the past 30 days
- include these keywords
- hit at least 300 likes

I then study the posts' structure, the hooks & CTA of these outliers... to improve mine.

→ Scripe

Great for sourcing content ideas.

It lets you aggregate posts by creator, making it simple to spot viral publications on any topic.

2. Copywriting

→ Grammarly

As a non-native English speaker, I make tons of spelling errors.

I use Grammarly's extension to correct my writing in real-time.

Not glamorous, but definitely essential in my LinkedIn stack.

→ Claude Code

I use Opus inside Claude Code to craft hook variations, repurpose older content and brainstorm new angles.

Since it holds so much context on my business, it fills in the gaps without me repeating the same info every time.

→ Conductor

My interface to run Claude Code (and Codex).

Makes writing and repurposing posts far easier thanks to their clean UI.

3. Visuals & Design

→ Figma / Adobe Suite

My LinkedIn strategy relies heavily on visuals.

When executed well, they amplify the reach of your publications by up to 10X.

Our designers use these to build our carousels and infographics.

→ Canva

My earliest carousels on here were built in Canva.

It's the most beginner-friendly app for creating social media visuals.

4. Video Creation

→ OpusClip

I use Opus's AI features to automatically produce short-form videos from my long-form content.

The output isn't as refined as a professional editor's...
But it's affordable and fast.

Multiple AI-edited videos landed me 50K+ views on LinkedIn.

→ Screen Studio

My go-to Mac screen recording app.

Perfect for video walkthroughs or tutorials because it automatically zooms and focuses on the parts of the screen you're working on.

It makes video outputs look noticeably more polished.

→ Ezgif

I use Ezgif to convert short audioless video content into .GIF files.

If you're posting a sub-15-second audio-less video on LinkedIn...

Speed it up and convert it to a GIF.

I'm not entirely sure why, but it outperforms on reach and engagement.

5. Content Planning

→ Notion

My central content hub.
It's where I collect ideas, draft them, and prep my posts.

Notion doubles as my content calendar.

That's the full stack I used to go from 0 to 70,000+ followers.

It's also the stack I plan to reach 100,000 in 2026.
